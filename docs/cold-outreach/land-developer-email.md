# Cold outreach — Day 13

Three cold emails, copy-paste, send Monday morning. Each uses
`{{firstName}}`, `{{company}}`, `{{city}}` placeholders — replace
with the actual recipient's details before sending.

The pattern is the same for all three: 4 sentences max,
"I'm building this, can I show you?", 15 minutes, no selling.
Each variant personalizes the vertical + the operator persona.

Before you send, you MUST:

- Replace `{{firstName}}` with their actual first name
- Replace `{{company}}` with their actual company
- Replace `{{city}}` with the city they care about (or remove the placeholder if it's a general ask)
- Sign as **David Nkana** (your name, not Chris — that's the AI's name)
- Send from your personal Gmail so the reply goes to your inbox

## Email 1 — Land developer (relationship-warm)

**Subject:** 15 minutes on land site selection in {{city}}?

```
Hi {{firstName}},

I've been building a tool that uses AI + real data to find land
for development in 30 seconds instead of 6 weeks. No scraping —
pulls from open data + Google Places. {{city}}, Sandton, Lusaka,
Pretoria all working today. Live at atlas-q2eh.vercel.app.

Are you the right person at {{company}} to show this to? If not,
who?

15 minutes this week?

David
```

**When to use:** When you have a personal connection to a land
developer who's been buying / selling in {{city}}. The "no
scraping" line is for the privacy-conscious operator who gets
asked about POPIA compliance by their legal team.

**Subject:** Where in {{city}} for the next acquisition?

```

warehouse, residential, civic — and shows the evidence behind
every score. OpenStreetMap, StatsSA census, live POI density.
Suburb-level decisions in 30 seconds, not 6 weeks.

Would 15 minutes this week work to walk through what we have
for {{city}}? Happy to send a screen recording if you'd rather
see it async.

David
```

**When to use:** When you're reaching a property investor or
REIT fund manager. They care about scale and edge — frame the
result in terms of how many sites they can evaluate per week
(10x is the magic number).

## How to use

1. Pick **3 real people** you actually know in the persona.
   Don't blast strangers — these are relationship-warm emails.
2. Replace `{{firstName}}`, `{{company}}`, `{{city}}` with
   the actuals. Read the email out loud before sending — if it
   doesn't sound like you, rewrite it.
3. Send Monday morning, 9-10am local time. Tuesday 9-10am is
   the second-best slot.
4. If no reply in 4 business days, follow up once: "Bumping
   this — saw the message below. Still interested in 15
   minutes?" Then stop. Never send a third email.
5. Whoever replies with even a soft "maybe", get them on a
   15-min call. Demo Atlas live. Ask: "What would make this
   worth R250/mo for you?"

## What success looks like

- **0 replies** → you sent to the wrong people. Adjust
  vertical or ask a friend for an introduction.
- **1 reply + 1 call** → that's 1 design partner. Get them
  to use Atlas for free for 1 month in exchange for weekly
  feedback. **This is your first customer.**
- **1 reply → paying customer** → R250-2,500/mo MRR.
  **This is your traction.**

## After 5 design partners

You'll have enough signal to:

1. Refine the product based on real use
2. Validate pricing (R250 vs R500? R2,500 vs R5,000?)
3. Have 5 customer testimonials for the investor pitch
4. Decide which vertical to deepen (gas station, restaurant,
   warehouse, retail?)

That's when you raise.

## Bonus: the 1-line reply

If a contact replies with a question, the only reply that
matters is the 1-liner that gets you a 15-min call:

```
Great question. Easier to show than tell — 15 mins Tuesday
or Wednesday? I'll send a calendar link once I know which.
```

Nothing else. The next message is the calendar link.
